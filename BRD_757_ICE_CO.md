## 8. PHYSICAL LOCATION — ROADMAP & ANALYSIS

### When to Pull the Trigger on Physical
**Don't open a physical location until:**
- Consistent $20,000+/month GMV online (proof of demand)
- You have a waitlist of 50+ customers wanting in-person service
- You have $30,000–$50,000 saved (6 months operating runway)
- Omar is doing jewelry full-time (not a side hustle)

Estimated timeline: **Month 12–18** (if online execution is strong)

### Location Analysis — Hampton Roads Options

#### Option 1: Hampton, VA (Recommended — Start Here)
**Why Hampton first:**
- Omar is already HERE — no commute, no relocation
- Hampton University is 2 miles from downtown — constant foot traffic
- Lower commercial rent than Virginia Beach or Norfolk
- Strong Black business community and local government support
- Phoebus neighborhood (downtown Hampton) is revitalizing — affordable storefronts

**Recommended area:** Phoebus District or Mercury Blvd corridor near Hampton University
**Est. commercial rent:** $800–$1,500/month for 400–800 sq ft
**Foot traffic:** Medium — needs heavy social media drive-to-store marketing
**Business license:** City of Hampton — straightforward, $50–$200/year

#### Option 2: Norfolk, VA (High Traffic, Higher Cost)
**Why consider Norfolk:**
- Granby Street / Ghent is the highest foot traffic retail corridor in Hampton Roads
- NSU is nearby; Navy base has constant military customer traffic
- Stronger nightlife scene (jewelry relevant for going out)
- MacArthur Center area or Granby Street — $1,500–$3,500/month rent

**Risk:** Higher overhead means higher break-even. Need $12,000+/month GMV to cover.
**Best for Phase 3** — after establishing brand with Hampton home base.

#### Option 3: Virginia Beach (Tourist Money, Seasonal)
**Why consider VB:**
- Summer tourism = massive cash influx May–September
- Atlantic Ave (the Strip) sees millions of visitors
- Affluent local population (Chesapeake/VB suburb money)
- Town Center area is upscale and established

**Risk:** Very seasonal — summer carries the year. Rent is highest in the region.
**Best for:** Pop-up summer shop, not a permanent location (yet).

#### Option 4: Richmond, VA (Long-Term Expansion Play)
**Why Richmond eventually:**
- 2nd largest city in Virginia, 1.3M metro population
- Strong HBCU presence (VSU, VUU), growing Black business scene
- Less saturated market than 757 for this niche
- 2.5-hour drive from Hampton — manageable for deliveries but not viable for daily ops

**Verdict:** Year 3–5 expansion, not a startup play.

### Studio-First Strategy (Smart Move Before Storefront)
Before a public storefront, run a **studio/appointment model:**
- Omar works from a dedicated workshop space (home, rented studio, or shared space)
- Customers come by appointment only — no walk-ins needed
- Overhead: $0–$400/month (if renting workshop space)
- Gives you 6–12 months of local in-person data before committing to a lease
- This is how most custom jewelers start and why they survive when mall jewelers close

**Key questions before signing a lease:**
1. Can you cover rent for 6 months with savings alone?
2. Are you averaging 20+ in-person customer inquiries/month?
3. Do you have a parking situation? (Hampton jewelry customers drive)
4. Is the location visible from the street or does it require directional signage?

## 11. SUPPLY CHAIN & SOURCING

### Wholesale Chain Sources
- **Alibaba/AliExpress:** Best for gold-filled chains in bulk. Search "18K gold filled Cuban link chain wholesale" — filter for 4+ star suppliers with 100+ orders. Sample first.
- **LA Jewelry District (Los Angeles):** 650+ jewelry wholesalers in one block. Can order via phone/email. Best prices in the US landed.
- **NYC Diamond District (47th St):** More diamond/stone focused. Good for moissanite settings.
- **DHgate:** Similar to Alibaba, good for watches and bracelet bases.
- **Stuller (stuller.com):** US-based, jeweler-grade findings and stone settings. Requires jeweler application.

### Gold for Grillz
- **10K gold:** ~$30–$50/dwt (pennyweight) from refinery or local scrap buyer
- **14K gold:** ~$45–$65/dwt
- Omar should establish a relationship with a local gold supplier (Norfolk or Richmond)
- Alternative: start with gold-filled molds (legal, still quality) and upgrade to solid as revenue grows

### Moissanite & Stone Sourcing
- **Charles & Colvard (moissanitecom):** Brand name moissanite, US supplier, good for marketing
- **MoissaniteCo:** Competitive pricing, good quality
- **eBay loose stones:** Risky but budget option for lower-tier pieces
