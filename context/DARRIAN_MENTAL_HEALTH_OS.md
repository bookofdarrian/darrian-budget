## 🧠 DBT Skills Darrian Learned (Voluntary Hospitalization, March 2026)

### Mindfulness — The Foundation
**"Mindfulness is living in the current moment. No room for rumination or future sight."**
- **WHAT skills**: Observe, Describe, Participate, Wordless Watching, Dropping into Pauses
- **HOW skills**: Nonjudgmentally, One-Mindfully, Effectively
- **Tools**: Meditation, baths, walks, showers, runs, making tea or coffee

### Wise Mind (The Core)
- **Emotion Mind** 🔥 = ruled by feelings, hot, mood-dependent
- **Reasonable Mind** 🧊 = ruled by logic, cool, task-focused
- **Wise Mind** 🌊 = the middle path; integration of both; inner wisdom

**Wise Mind practices Darrian learned:**
1. Stone on the Lake — imagine floating down to your center
2. Walking the Spiral Staircase — slowly descend within yourself
3. Breathing "Wise In / Mind Out"
4. Asking Wise Mind a question — listen, don't give yourself the answer
5. Dropping into the pauses between breaths

### Distress Tolerance — IMPROVE
- **I** — Imagery (safe place, emotions draining out)
- **M** — Meaning (find purpose in the pain)
- **P** — Prayer / Meditation / Spiritual practice
- **R** — Relaxation (hot bath, deep breathing, yoga)
- **O** — One thing in the moment (One-Mindfully)
- **V** — Vacation, brief (1 hour. Pull the covers up. It's OK.)
- **E** — Self-Encouragement ("This too shall pass. I will make it out.")

### TIPP — When Emotions Are Overwhelming
- **T** — Temperature (cold water, ice on wrists)
- **I** — Intense Exercise (20 jumping jacks, sprint in place)
- **P** — Paced Breathing (elongated exhale, 4 in / 8 out)
- **P** — Paired Muscle Relaxation (tense → hold → release)

### STOP Skill — Before Reacting
- **S** — Stop. Don't react.
- **T** — Take a breath.
- **O** — Observe what's happening.
- **P** — Proceed mindfully.

### One-Mindfully (ADHD + Bipolar specific)
> "When eating, eat. When walking, walk. When working, just work. When remembering, just remember."
- Do ONE thing at a time. This is the antidote to time blindness and emotional flooding.
- Awareness at all times when trying to be mindful is key.

### Radical Acceptance
- Accept reality as it is — THEN you can change it
- Acceptance is not approval. It's acknowledging what IS.
- "Changing reality first vs accepting reality" — Darrian's own note

### Walking the Middle Path
- Balance Reasonable Mind with Emotion Mind → Wise Mind
- Balance doing mind with being mind
- Balance desire for change with radical acceptance
- Balance self-denial with self-indulgence
