## PLATFORM INTELLIGENCE

### StockX
- Highest liquidity for GR (general release) and popular hyped silhouettes
- Strict authentication required; condition must be DS (deadstock)
- Fees: ~9.5% seller fee + $13.95 payment processing (tiered by seller level)
- Best for: Jordan 1s, Nike Dunks, Yeezys, NB 550s, New Balances with hype
- Avoid: Deadstock only — no worn or VNDS here

### GOAT
- Mix of DS and used; broader buyer base
- Fees: ~9.5% + $5 cash out fee; lower for consignment sellers
- Better for: Used/VNDS pairs, rare sizes (low/high), vintage
- Has direct offer feature — great for moving slow inventory

### eBay
- Most flexible platform — DS, used, bundles, lots, international
- Fees: ~12.9% + $0.30 per transaction
- Best for: Vintage, rare, obscure, bundle lots, international buyers
- Listings drive organic SEO — invest in descriptions and photos

### Poshmark
- Growing sneaker category; mostly used/VNDS audience
- Fees: flat $2.95 under $15, 20% over $15
- Best for: Apparel bundles, women's/kids' sneakers, casual releases

### Facebook Marketplace / Local
- Zero fees; highest margin
- Best for: Quick local flips, heavy shoes (avoid high shipping), urgent cash
- Community-first platform — treat buyers fairly, build local reputation

### Craigslist
- Cash only; meet safely (public places only)
- Best for: Bulk lots, high-value pairs to trusted local buyers

## PRICING INTELLIGENCE

### Price Discovery Hierarchy
1. StockX last sale (most recent 5 sales) — ground truth for liquid pairs
2. GOAT active asks (lowest ask) — ceiling for DS pricing
3. eBay sold listings (last 90 days, sold filter ON) — realistic range for used/VNDS
4. Facebook Marketplace comps (same city, 30 days) — local floor

### Margin Framework
Target margins by category:
- GR Hype (Jordan 1, Dunk): 15-25% above cost after fees
- Limited/Collab: 30-50%+ (hold for peak; watch sell-through velocity)
- Vintage: 40-100%+ (patience required; illiquid market)
- GR Non-Hype: 5-10% (volume play only; assess carefully)
- Dead stock you can't move: cut losses at break-even + 5% over platform fees

### When to Hold vs. Sell
- Hold: pairs with active hype cycle (collab drops, restocks, media moments)
- Hold: limited inventory that appreciates (confirmed limited run, no restock)
- Sell NOW: anything 90+ days old with no price appreciation
- Sell NOW: anything tying up >$300 in capital with no clear buyer path
- Never hold: GR Nike/Jordan with confirmed restocks coming

## LISTING TITLE FORMULA

**eBay/GOAT title structure:**
`[Brand] [Model] [Colorway/SKU] [Size] [Condition]`

Examples:
- `Nike Air Jordan 1 High OG "Chicago" 555088-101 Size 10 DS New`
- `New Balance 550 White Green BB550WT1 Size 9.5 VNDS 9/10`
- `Adidas Yeezy 350 V2 Zebra CP9654 Size 11 Deadstock`

**StockX:** Uses model + colorway only (auto-populated from catalog)

**Tips:**
- Include SKU/style code — this increases search match and buyer confidence
- Condition matters: DS/New, VNDS 9/10, Used 8/10 — be specific and honest
- Photos > titles for conversion. 8 photos minimum. Natural light preferred.
- Dishonest condition descriptions = returns + account strikes. Never fudge it.

## SOURCING INTELLIGENCE

### Primary Channels
1. **Nike SNKRS / Adidas App** — raffle and draw entries for hype releases
2. **FootLocker / Champs / JD Sports** — GR releases; use RFID notifications
3. **Local Resellers** — buy lots at wholesale; build relationships
4. **Estate Sales / Thrift** — vintage hunting; inconsistent but highest margin
5. **Nike Outlet / Adidas Outlet** — below-retail finds for patient resellers

### Arbitrage Opportunities
- **Size arbitrage**: Buy common sizes cheap, sell rare sizes premium (size 6, 13, 14+)
- **Regional arbitrage**: Pairs that move locally may not move online (and vice versa)
- **Timing arbitrage**: Buy at retail fatigue (2-4 weeks post-drop), sell at media moments
- **Condition arbitrage**: VNDS pairs on eBay → clean, relist with better photos + higher price

### Red Flags — Do Not Source
- Pairs with confirmed restocks incoming (GR Air Force 1s, basic Dunks, etc.)
- Pairs in sizes you can't verify are authentic
- Lots from sellers who can't provide receipts for >$500 batches
- Anything that requires authentication you're not confident in

## INVENTORY HEALTH FRAMEWORK

Score every pair in inventory:
- **Green**: < 30 days, at or above cost + margin target
- **Yellow**: 30-60 days, stagnant or price dropped once
- **Red**: 60-90 days, stuck, needs repricing or platform switch
- **Dead**: 90+ days, cut losses, liquidate or donate

Review inventory health every Monday. Never let red inventory become dead.
